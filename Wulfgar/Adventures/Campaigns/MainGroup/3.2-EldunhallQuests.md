## Quests of Eldunhall
In Eldunhall the party can engage with the following quests

### City of Eldunhall
Within the city of Eldunhall the party can find the following important locations.

#### Saldrinsen House
One of the largest houses of Eldunhall, the Saldrisen Home is a large building adorned with paintings of hammers, anvils, and fire...

***Kathra Saldrisen.***
Kathra is a 4'3'' tall dwarven woman in her late 70's with long red hair, a shallow beard, and dark brown eyes. She wears a long green dress adorned with small brass flowers.

Kathra is currently the matriarch of the family and has been overworked to the point of exhaustion, recently she has delegated some of her responsibilities to Durin's uncle Viktor. 


***Viktor Saldrisen.***
Viktor is a 4'7'' tall dwarf in his early 70's with short dark brown hair, a long braided beard with steel rings adorning it, a large nose, and inviting green eyes. He wears a green gambeson adorned with hammer symbols.

Viktor has been acting as the diplomat of the Saldrisen family, arranging deals with other families. He is the brother of Durin's father and is biding his time to become the patriarch of the family.


#### Temple of Memories
The temple of memories is a octagonal building  with two archways per wall, located in the center of the city. Within the party can find a central platform with statues dedicated to the various gods.

The temple is the domain of the Asleson family, a branch of the Armura family from Kragmeer.


#### Artisan's Hall
The artisan's hall is a long corridor within the fortress-city that is subdivided into the various guilds of the city.

### The Council of Elders
Durin then is invited into a meeting with the elders of Eldunhall where he must present his goals and satisfy the elders...

#### Chamber of Lords
The elders of Eldunhall make their meetings in a large chamber within the Groontenfjell mountain...

The octagonal shaped room has a two-teared elevated platform on the opposite side of the entrance where the elders sit on thrones of marble with fine pelts and linen cushions.


***Trym Eldunsen.***
The eldest of Eldunhall, Trym is a 4'8'' tall blind dwarf with pale wrinkly skin, a long braided white beard that is adorned with golden rings. He wears a ornamented red and gray gambeson with green leaves, a plated belt, and a golden crown.

Trym is the wisest of the elders, he always waits all opinions to be posed before he utters a single word.


***Stiga Vihrkaasi.***
Stiga is a 4'4'' tall dwarven woman with milk-white skin, a shallow but well kept beard, long brown hair adorned with silver laurels, and brown eyes. She wears a long red dress and a green cloak.

Stiga is the matriarch of the largest farmer family and responsible for all the food of the city and surrounding towns and villages. She is a wise but worried elder that regards all the citizens of Eldunhall and its surrounding areas as her "children".


***Tore Asleson.***
Tore is a 4'5'' tall dwarf with rugged and scared skin, stern dark brown eyes, a long beard, and a hooked nose. He wears a set of plate armor adorned with brass ornaments that depict hammers.

Tore is the commander of the elder guard and the patriarch of the most militaristic family of Eldunhall. He was friends with Durin's father but he found Durin's voyage foolish. He is struggling with a rising criminality in and around the city.


***Arnstein Hoem.***
Arnstein is a 4'7'' tall dwarf with frail white skin, dark blue eyes, a long white beard, long white hair, and a metal band around his forehead. He wears a checker black and green tunic under a brown bear cloak.

Arnstein is the lore master and religious leader of the city of Eldunhall, he despises the Saldrinsen family as he sees their search of the past a attack at his power.

### Cave of Darkness
After discussing with the elders of Eldunhall, the party is challenged to investigate a cave that has been isolated after two groups of miners disappeared. In reality the collective fears of the creatures that may lurk within the deeper caves has coalesced a small section of the shallowest layer of the Astral world, bringing creatures such as Kobolds.

The miners delved into the cave, reached the lowest layer of the Astral world and were attacked by the Kobolds, killing them and offering their bodies to their king.

#### Normal Cave
At first the party enters a simple mine that seems to be only slightly abandoned but still its full of dangers, roll on the events sequence table to determine the order of events.

##### Order of Events Table
| 1d6 | Order of Events                               |
|:---:|:----------------------------------------------|
| 1-2 | Resting Point, Deadly Cliff, Body in the Dark |
| 3-4 | Resting Point, Rocks Fall, Small Clue         |
| 5-6 | Resting Point, Narrow Passage, Small Clue     |

___
The following are the possible events that can occur while the party is traveling down the mine.

***Resting Point.***
The party finds a small room with beds and chests carved into the wall of the mine, within it they can find 5 torches, a pouch containing 5 gp and 7 sp, a letter to Narki Fjellfar, and a small stone statue depicting Kaur. With a Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Perception) check a character can discern that the room was the last resting point of the crew and that no fight occurred in the room.


***Narrow Passage.***
The party reaches a point where the cave narrows drastically, each creature creature Medium or larger must make a DC 15 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or take 2 (1d4) Slashing damage.


***Rocks Fall.***
The party reaches a large chamber with various stalagmites and stalactites, as they walk through the room it shakes causing rocks to fall, each character must make a DC 13 Dexteity (Acrobatics) or Strength (Athletics) check, on a failed check the creature takes 3 (1d6) Bludgeoning damage.


***Deadly Cliff.***
The party reaches a underground ravine with a narrow passageway besides a 60 foot fall. Each creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check, on a failed check the creature starts to fall but can be aided by another character. If all fails the character falls 60 feet, taking 17 (5d6) Bludgeoning damage.

If a creature survives the fall it can find a **Flame Stone Plate Ring**.

>
> ***Fire Resistance.***
> You gain resistance to fire damage.
>
> Whenever you suffer fire damage a scale like growth spreads from the finger of the ring. Breaking the attunement to this item doesn't remove the growth.
>

***Body in the Dark.***
The party reaches a small chamber with a body of one of the miners. He seems to have died from violent slashes. With a successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Medicine) check a character can determine that the miner died from a poison as much as the wounds.

***Small Clue.***
The party reaches a small chamber with a small pile of strange scale-like rocks in the middle of the room. With a successful DC 17 Intelligence (Arcana) or Intelligence (Nature) check a character can determine that the scales are not naturally occurring object.


#### Clipoff
After a few more chambers the party reaches a narrow corridor, characters with the *Spellcasting* start to feel uneasy. As the party continues down their path they start to see strange mushrooms that glow with a blue hue, bright red snails, various pairs of glowing lights in the darkness.

The party then reaches a narrow corridor (10 ft. wide, 60 ft long, 10 ft high) with a small dragon-humanoid stone statue in the middle, if a character approaches the "statue" (Kobold) it opens its mouth and attacks with its **Cobalt Breath**. Then four more Kobolds climb down the walls surrounding the party. Roll **Initiative!**


***Kobold.***
The kobold is a small humanoid with the head of a dragon, a grayish blue skin composed of lizard-like scales, sunken eyes that glow yellow, metallic blue fangs and claws that allows it to climb the rocks of the deep caves, and a breath that spews poisonous grey cloud.
